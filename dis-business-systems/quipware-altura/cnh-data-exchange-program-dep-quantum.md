_Provides details on the DEP (Data Exchange Program) offered by CNH._

Introduction 

 The Data Exchange Program (DEP) lets Quantum dealerships at NR116 or higher share their CNH service history with CNH and in exchange have access to all unit data previously submitted to CNH from any other participating dealership. That data is presented to users in the Expanded View pane in Quantum to create an efficient service workflow. 

 This article: 

 Details the Onboarding process 
 Provides instruction for accessing the DEP information through P.O.S. Invoicing 
 Breaks down the information provided in the following data points including how to use available SRT codes: 
 Unit Owner Information 
 Unit Information (including warranty dates) 
 Service History 
 Warranty Vehicle Lookup 
 Purchased Protection Plan (PPP) Information 
 Telematics Data with map 
 Performance Improvement Program (PiP) or Campaign Information 
 Service History with DMS Information 
 Standard Repair Times (SRT) Lookup 
 Fault Codes 

 This is a purchasable product. Contact DIS at 1-800-426-8870 or Sales@discorp.com for more information. 

  

 Onboarding Process Overview 

 Upon purchase, you are onboarded by a DIS Representative where you will complete the process below. 

 

 Access Altura and select DEP Configurations . 
 Create or enter CNH credentials that have eWarranty access. 

 Note : The password has a reset feature that automatically updates the password every 50 – 55 days to ensure it is secure and up to date. 

 Select Save Changes . 
 Select Agree on the DocuSign document that displays the first time this process is completed. 

  

  

 View and/or Download CNH DEP Information 

 Select Invoicing (P.O.S. | Invoicing) , create the invoice and proceed to the Sold To screen.  

 

 Select the Unit Info tab. 
 Provide the Serial Number for the unit. If the unit is already in the system, you can click the Serial # button and select it from the list that appears. 
 Click Enter . If the Serial Number is assigned  to a Case Make, the Expanded View tab becomes selectable on the right side of the screen. 
 Click the Expanded View tab. 

 The Expanded View Pane expands listing the Unit Owner information from the Expanded View Home page by default. Click the Home icon (underlined below) to return to this screen from any Expanded View menu option.

 Section A Data Points                           ( Homepage ) 

 Unit Owner Information – This section displays by default when the Expanded View pane is opened or the Homepage icon is selected. It includes the name of the unit owner and their location as known by CNH. 

 

 Click the Owner caret to expand or collapse the unit owner information. 

 Unit Information (including warranty dates) 

 Click the Unit Information caret to display basic information about the unit including the warranty start date. 

 

 Service History 

 Click the Service History caret to display unit claim history that has been provided to CNH. The information shown through this menu option can also be viewed by clicking the Service History option at the top of the Expanded View pane. 

 

 Click the Refresh button to include the most recent service history. 
 Search for previous service history by entering full or partial claim numbers and complaints, then clicking the Magnifying Glass to list each instance of service history with matching information. 
 If there are several instances of recorded service history, click the Page carets to scroll backwards and forwards through the pages of service history. 
 Click on the Information icon (i in blue circle) to view all of the available details regarding that specific unit service history including: 

 Dealer Claim number 
 Dealer Name 
 Date of service 
 Warranty information 
 Description of unit issue requiring service 

 Warranty  / Warranty Vehicle Lookup 

 Click the Warranty caret to display each warranty purchased for the unit as reported to CNH. This information can also be viewed by clicking the Warranty option at the top of the Expanded pane. 

 

 Click the Refresh button to ensure the most recent warranty information is included. 
 Click the Active toggle switch to display Active or Inactive warranties. As shown in the second screenshot above, an X in a red circle precedes warranties that are no longer active. 

 PPP (Purchased Protection Plan) Information 

 Click the PPP caret to display any additional 3 rd party warranty programs for the unit. These flexible plans usually cover items not covered under standard warranties and are transferred with sold units.

 Selecting this menu option displays a list of other selectable menu options that allow you to view (DMS) information about the customer and unit as recorded by your dealership.

 Campaign (PiP) Information 

 Click the Campaign option at the top of the Expanded pane to view active and inactive campaigns (product improvement programs) for the unit. Campaigns are often structured proactive programs intended to address specific unit performance issues or updates. 

 

 Click the Active switch to toggle between current and expired campaigns. 
 Click the Magnifying Glass to present a search field. You can search campaigns using partial or full campaign codes, names, or descriptions. 
 Click the Information icon (i in a blue circle) for any campaign to reveal additional details about that campaign. 

 Service History with DMS Information 

 Click the Service History option at the top of the Expanded View pane to display service history for the unit. When Service History is selected, the Manufacturer Service History displays by default. 

 

 Click the Refresh button to include the most recent service history. 
 Search for previous service history by entering full or partial claim numbers and complaints, and then clicking the Magnifying Glass to list each instance of service history with matching information. 
 Click on the Information icon (i in blue circle) to view all of the available details regarding the unit service history including: 

 Dealer Claim number 
 Dealer Name 
 Date of service 
 Warranty information 
 Description of unit issue requiring service 

 Click the Service History – DMS option to see the unit’s service history at your dealership. By default, the service history is sorted by Work Order as indicated by the arrow next to the header. 

 

 Click the Filter button to set and apply or remove existing filters. A filter can be set to only display service history from a beginning date (as shown above) and/or before a specific date. 

 A red circle on the Filter button shows if any filters are set and how many. 

 Click Clear to remove a set filter or Save Filter if you want to re-apply the filter at a later time. 

 Click the Sort By button to specify how service history is displayed in the table. As shown above, an arrow displays next to the header to indicate the currently applied Sort By method. 
 Click in the Search field and search for specific service history based on the provided description. 
 Click on a hyperlinked Work Order or Work Order Segment to view associated details. 

 Standard Repair Time (SRT) Code Lookup 

 Click the SRT option in the Expanded pane to view CNH provided SRT Codes associated with the unit. These codes include the estimated time to complete specific repairs on the unit and can be used to standardize labor rates and ensure consistency in billing. SRT codes help streamline warranty claims and job scheduling. 

 

 Select Equipment or Parts to display those types of SRT codes provided by CNH for the unit. 
 Click the caret for the Group from which you would like to select the SRT code. Each Group may have Subgroups and Sub-Subgroups from which SRT codes can be selected. 

 Select the check box for the specific SRT code(s) you want to use on the work order. 

 Once you have selected all of the desired check boxes, click the Selected SRT button in the upper right corner. The Add to WO screen opens listing the selected SRTs. 
 Click the Add to WO button to add the SRTs to the Work Order. A green confirmation banner displays at the bottom of the Expanded pane. 

 Note : You can click the Red trashcan to remove SRTs from the list or click the Back to Add Screen if you need to add additional SRTs. 

 A message appears on the Detail screen in Quantum stating that New SRT codes exist for the segment they are applied to on the work order. 

 

 Access the work order Detail screen. Select the note line associated with the SRT code, click the Navigation button and select Notes . 
 Click the Import SRT Codes button from the Work with Notes screen, then click the Return with Changes button. The Calculate Labor Flat Rates screen displays. 

 

 Do one of the following: 

 Select the Do Not Calculate Labor Flat Rates button to bring the SRT code into the work order as a note line. 
 Select the Calculate Labor Flat Rates button to create a flat rate for the maintenance procedure. The Calculate Labor Flat Rates screen displays. Configure the flat rate as desired. 

 Fault Codes 

 Click the Fault Codes option at the top of the Expanded pane. If a modem is installed on the unit, any fault codes from the previous 30 days are listed by default. 

 

 Click on the Start Date and End Date fields to set the date range to search for issued fault codes. The start and end date MUST be within 30 days of each other. 
 Click the refresh button to search the provided date range for fault codes and ensure the listed fault code information is current. 
 Use the search field to locate a specific fault code.
