_Setup Scheduled Maintenance for a Unit - Quantum_

Introduction 

 The Inquire/Update Service Units option allows you to configure a customer’s unit for scheduled maintenance and add associated information. Information that can be added includes: 

 Unit make/model/year and other information 
 Warranty information 
 Service Notes 
 Information for Service Maintenance scheduled 

 These instructions assume that the unit and customer are currently in the system. 

 If you need to add the customer, click here   for instructions. For a cash only customer, click  here . 

 If you need to add a customer's unit/equipment, click  here  for instructions. 

  

 To Setup Scheduled Maintenance for a Unit 

 Select  Maintain Unit Service Info (Service | Service Utilities | Maintain Unit Service Info) . The Maintain Unit Service Info screen opens. 

    

 Type the address number for the customer that you want to setup scheduled maintenance for in the Address # field above the table and click  Enter . All units in the system that are owned by the customer are listed beginning at the top of the table. 

 Choose the unit that you want to setup scheduled maintenance for and click the  Select  button. The Display Unit Service Info screen opens. 

 

 Click the  Edit  button. The second Maintain Unit Service Info screen opens. 

     

 Provide entries in the following fields when applicable: 

 Inst Date - If the scheduled maintenance is associated with an installed part, enter the installation date in the Inst Date field. 
 90 Day, 1/3/5 year, CFPM End – If the installed part has a warranty that falls under one of these categories, enter the end date in the respective field. 
 SM Type – FSM for Full Service Maintenance or ISM for Inspection Service Maintenance. 
 SM Freq – The frequency of the maintenance. 
 SM Due Type – Engine Hours or Date. 
 SM Rate – Flat rate to be charged. 
 Cons Rate – Hourly rate for consumables. 
 SM Due – Date next scheduled maintenance is due. 
 SM Prev – The last service date 
 Labor Hours – The amount of time expected to complete the job. 
 SM Due Hr – The engine hour reading when the scheduled maintenance is due. 
 SM Prev Hr – The engine hour reading when the scheduled maintenance was last performed. 
 Contract # – The number for the contract assigned to this unit. 

 Click  Enter  to save the information. A message appears across the bottom of the screen stating the maintenance Record changed. 

 Click  Exit  to return to the main menu.
